Visualizing Uneven Public Safety

NYC Shooting Redistribution by Neighborhood, 2016–2025

Compare shooting incidents across NYC neighborhoods by selecting a baseline and comparison year. Each point represents one Neighborhood Tabulation Area (NTA). Hover to view shooting change, neighborhood race/ethnicity, and employment context.

Interpretive caution: demographic overlays describe neighborhood population context, not the race or ethnicity of shooting victims or offenders.
Loading live data. Fetching shooting incidents and 2010 NTA boundaries from live URLs. Neighborhood race/ethnicity demographics are loaded from ACS tract data and aggregated to NTAs for the selected comparison year. This can take a little time because the file spatially assigns incidents to NTA polygons in your browser.
How to read the chart: the diagonal line marks no change. Points below the line had fewer shootings in the comparison year than the baseline year. Points above the line had more shootings. Points far from the line show larger changes. Use the dropdowns to compare years and filter by borough or category.
Why 2021 and 2025? The dashboard defaults to 2021 and 2025 to compare the post-pandemic shooting surge with the most recent full year of incident-level data.

Sources: NYPD Shootings (2006–Present), NYC Open Data; 2010 Neighborhood Tabulation Areas, WGS84 GeoJSON boundary file; ACS race/ethnicity data aggregated from census tracts to NTAs using the NYC tract-to-NTA equivalency table; ACS-style employment context keyed to the selected comparison year. Race/ethnicity in the hover overlay describes neighborhood population demographics, not victim or offender race.

Limitations: shooting incident counts do not measure all forms of community harm, fear, trauma, underreporting, or residents’ perceptions of safety. Counts should be interpreted alongside community conditions and local prevention capacity.
